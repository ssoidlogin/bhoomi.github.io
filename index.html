Bhoomi

Bhoomi is the official digital land records management system launched by the Government of Karnataka. It was created to digitize paper records, making them transparent, tamper-proof, and easily accessible to citizens.

The most important document you can access here is the RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops), locally known as Pahani. Whether you are a farmer applying for a loan or a landowner verifying property details, the Bhoomi portal eliminates the need to visit government offices, allowing you to access records from your home.

Why is the RTC (Pahani) Document Important?

The RTC contains vital details about a specific piece of land:

  • Owner Details: Who currently owns the land.
  • Tenancy: Who is cultivating the land.
  • Crops: What crops are grown (essential for crop insurance).
  • Land Type: Agricultural, commercial, or non-agricultural.
  • Liabilities: Existing bank loans or litigations on the land.

How to Check Bhoomi RTC (Pahani) Online

Follow this step-by-step guide to view your land records on the Karnataka Bhoomi portal.

Step 1: Visit the Portal
Go to the official Bhoomi Land Records website (landrecords.karnataka.gov.in).

Step 2: Select 'View RTC and MR'
On the homepage, look for the "Services" section and click on "View RTC and MR".

Step 3: Enter Land Details
You will be asked to fill in the following details based on where your land is located: District, Taluk, Hobli, Village.

Step 4: Survey Number
Enter the Survey Number of your land. Once entered, the system will ask for the Surnoc and Hissa Number (sub-division number).

Step 5: Fetch Details
Click on "Fetch Details". The complete land record (RTC) will be displayed on your screen. You can view owner names, land extent, and soil type here.

Note: To download a signed legal copy for court or loan purposes, you must use the "i-RTC" wallet service and pay a nominal fee (approx. ₹15).

What is a Digitally Signed i-RTC?

Unlike the informational copy, a Digitally Signed i-RTC is a legal document valid for court proceedings, bank loan applications, and property registration. It carries a unique QR code and digital signature that verifies its authenticity.

Steps to Download Signed i-RTC:

  1. Login: Visit the Bhoomi portal and select "i-RTC Wallet Services". You must log in with your User ID (or create a new account).
  2. Add Money: The signed copy is a paid service (approx. ₹15 per copy). You must top up your wallet using UPI, Net Banking, or Credit Card.
  3. Search Land: Enter your District, Taluk, Hobli, Village, and Survey Number.
  4. Pay & Print: Click "Pay and Print." The PDF will download immediately.

Save this PDF securely; the download link may expire.

How to Check Mutation Status (MR) Online

If you have recently bought or sold land, you need to check if the ownership transfer (Mutation) has been updated in the government records.

  • Navigate to the "View RTC and MR" section.
  • Select the "Mutation Status" tab.
  • Enter the District, Taluk, Hobli, Village, and Survey Number.
  • Click "Fetch Details".

The system will show the current status of your mutation application (e.g., Pending, Accepted, or Rejected).

How to View Village Revenue Maps Online?

A Revenue Map shows the geographical boundaries of a village and the division of survey numbers within it. It helps landowners identify the shape and location of their plot within the village layout.

How to Download:

  1. Go to the "Revenue Maps" section on the Bhoomi home page.
  2. Select your District, Taluk, and Hobli.
  3. Choose your Village from the list.
  4. Click the PDF icon to download the full village map.

These maps are large files; ensure you have a stable internet connection.

What is the Dishaank App?

Developed by the Karnataka Remote Sensing Applications Centre (KSRSAC), the Dishaank App allows you to see your survey number based on your current GPS location. This is incredibly useful for verifying if a plot of land you are buying actually falls under the survey number claimed by the seller.

How to Use:

  • Download Dishaank from the Google Play Store.
  • Enable GPS/Location services on your phone.
  • Stand on the land in question and open the app.
  • The map will overlay the survey boundaries on your location, showing you the exact Survey Number and Gomala (government land) details.

How to Verify XML RTC

Why Verify? Fraudsters can alter printed documents. The "Verify XML" feature ensures that the document you hold matches the data on the government server exactly.

Steps:

  1. Go to the "RTC XML Verification" tab on the portal.
  2. Enter the Reference Number found on your downloaded i-RTC.
  3. The system will confirm if the document is genuine and display the valid data for comparison.

Technical & Access Issues

The portal handles massive traffic, which can lead to slow loading times or "Server Not Found" errors.

  • Browser Compatibility: The website works best on Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox. Avoid using older versions of Internet Explorer.
  • Clear Cache: If the page isn't loading correctly or buttons aren't clicking, clear your browser’s Cache and Cookies (Ctrl+Shift+Delete) and restart the browser.
  • Pop-up Blockers: The RTC is usually generated in a new window. If nothing happens when you click "View" or "Download," check your browser’s address bar for a blocked pop-up icon and select "Always allow."
  • OTP Not Received: During registration or login, if you don't receive an OTP, wait 2 minutes before clicking "Resend." Ensure your mobile number is not on a DND (Do Not Disturb) list.

Payment & Download Issues

These are the most common frustrations, especially when money is debited but the document doesn't appear.

Issue Solution
Money Deducted, No PDF Go to the "Missed i-RTC" section on the portal. Enter your order number or transaction details to re-download the document without paying again.
Payment Status "Pending" This usually clears within 24 hours. Avoid making a second payment immediately. Check the "Check Payment Status" tool on the site.
PDF Not Opening Ensure you have a PDF reader (like Adobe Acrobat) installed. If the file is 0KB, the download was interrupted; try again from the "Missed i-RTC" section.

Data & Record Issues

Sometimes the website works fine, but the information displayed is incorrect or missing.

  • Survey Number Not Found: Ensure you are entering the Surnoc and Hissa numbers correctly.
    • Try searching by Owner Name instead of Survey Number to see if the record is indexed differently.
  • Name or Measurement Mismatch: If the owner's name is spelled wrong or the land area is incorrect, this cannot be fixed online. You must visit the Tahsildar office (Taluk level) with your original Sale Deed to apply for a Rectification Mutation.
  • Mutation Status "Pending": If you recently bought land, it takes roughly 45–60 days for the mutation process to reflect on the RTC. You can track this under the "Mutation Status" tab using your transaction ID.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Can I use the online RTC copy for legal purposes?
A: The free viewable version is for information only. For legal matters, bank loans, or registration, you must download the Digitally Signed i-RTC, which is valid without a manual signature.

Q: What if my land details are missing on Bhoomi?
A: If your data is not digitized or contains errors, you must visit the nearest Bhoomi Monitoring Cell or your Taluk office to file an application for data entry or correction.

Q: How can I view the Revenue Map of my village?
A: Visit the "Revenue Maps" section on the Bhoomi portal, select your district/taluk/village, and you can download the PDF map showing survey number boundaries.
